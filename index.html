About Me

AI developer and firmware engineer.

As a seasoned AI developer and firmware engineer, I am deeply passionate about pioneering innovative solutions that bridge the realms of artificial intelligence and firmware architectures. Over the past seven years, I have honed my expertise in crafting and optimizing AI models for diverse applications, with a primary focus on image processing and computer vision.

My journey encompasses a rich tapestry of experiences, ranging from leading the development of object detection and tracking systems for surveillance applications to delving into the intricacies of mmWave radar data collection. I thrive on challenges and have successfully undertaken comprehensive projects, such as designing an automatic bottle filling system and emulating a toy car with the Carla simulator.

In academia, I earned a Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering, specializing in information retrieval from biomedical images for early detection of neuro diseases. My commitment to advancing knowledge is evident in my research contributions, including published articles in reputable journals and conferences, covering topics such as stroke lesion detection, multiple sclerosis lesion segmentation, and license plate recognition.

My technical proficiency extends across an array of technologies, including machine learning, deep learning, embedded systems (Arduino, ESP32, Raspberry Pi), and programming languages (C, C++, Python). I am adept at leveraging frameworks such as TensorFlow, PyTorch, and OpenCV to create robust and efficient solutions.
